## What this policy covers

This policy describes how we handle personal data when you:

- Install or use the KinBridge Support Android application ("the app")
- Sign in or sign up via the KinBridge dashboard at
  [kinbridge.support](https://kinbridge.support) ("the dashboard")
- Receive or provide help through a KinBridge session

It does **not** cover third-party services you reach through KinBridge
(e.g. if a helper opens a banking app on your phone during a session —
that app has its own privacy policy).

## At a glance

- We collect the **minimum** we need to run the service.
- We **do not sell** your data, ever.
- We **do not show ads** and do not use advertising identifiers.
- Screen content shared during a session is visible in real time to

- You can **delete your account** at any time. When you do, we delete
  your data on a schedule described below.
- The app uses two sensitive Android permissions — **MediaProjection**
  and **Accessibility Service** — that are explained below.

## 1. Personal data we collect

### 1.1 Data you provide directly

- **Account data.** Email address, password (hashed — we never see your
  plaintext password), optional display name, optional avatar image.
  Collected when you sign up on the dashboard.
- **Device name.** When you register a device (e.g. "Mom's Pixel 8"), we
  store the name you choose.
- **Pairing codes.** Short-lived 6-digit codes you generate to invite a
  helper. Stored as a hash; the plaintext code exists only in the
  dashboard UI for its {{PAIRING_CODE_TTL}}-minute lifetime.
- **Optional 2FA (TOTP).** If you enable two-factor authentication, we
  store a TOTP factor identifier. We never see your TOTP seed in
  plaintext after enrollment.

### 1.2 Data generated by your use of the service

- **Session metadata.** Which device, which helper, when the session
  started, when it ended, how long it lasted, who ended it.
- **Session audit trail.** Every tap, scroll, screenshot, or note
  written during a session is recorded as a `session_event` so you can
  review what happened. Payload shape depends on event type; examples:
  `{ target: "Settings" }` for a tap, `{ title: "Connected to Wi-Fi" }`
  for a note.
- **Chat messages.** Text messages sent between helper and owner during
  a session.
- **Device presence.** A timestamp indicating the last time your device
  contacted our servers, used to render "online" indicators.

### 1.3 Data collected automatically by the infrastructure

- **IP addresses.** Recorded in server logs for security and abuse
  detection. Retained for {{IP_LOG_RETENTION_DAYS}} days.
- **Connection metadata.** Packet headers, relay routing information.
  Required for the WebRTC / RustDesk transport to function. Not stored
  beyond the session's duration.
- **Crash diagnostics.** If the app crashes, anonymous crash reports
  may be collected by the Play Store's built-in mechanism (Android
  Vitals). We do not deploy a third-party crash SDK.

### 1.4 Data we do **not** collect

- **Ad identifiers.** We don't read the Android Advertising ID.
- **Contacts, calendar, call log, SMS.** The app never requests these.
- **Location.** We do not request location permission. We may infer
  approximate location from your IP address for abuse detection, but
  we don't store precise location.
- **Microphone or camera** (outside of active sessions with explicit
  permission). The "Voice" tool in the live-session overlay activates
  the mic only while a session is active and the tool is selected.

### 1.5 Data from sensitive Android permissions

KinBridge requests two sensitive permissions that bear special
disclosure under Google Play policy:

- **MediaProjection** (screen capture). Activated only when the owner
  explicitly consents to a session and Android shows its standard
  system dialog. Screen frames are transmitted in real time to the
  approved helper's device and are **not** recorded or stored by us.
  Screenshots the helper saves during the session are recorded as
  `screenshot` events but the image bytes are not persisted by default
  — if the helper saves a screenshot, we retain it only for the
  **session's audit trail** and delete it on session deletion.
- **Accessibility Service** (tap and input injection). Used only on
  the *owner's* device to let the helper perform taps the owner is
  watching. No data is extracted from other apps via the Accessibility
  API; we do not read the contents of other apps, clipboard, or
  notifications.

## 2. How we use personal data

- **To provide the service.** Route your sessions, render dashboards,
  authenticate you, maintain pairings.
- **To keep you informed.** Deliver notifications about session
  activity (when you've enabled them).
- **To keep the service secure.** Detect abuse, rate-limit brute-force
  attempts, enforce our Terms.
- **To comply with law.** Respond to valid legal requests, retain
  transaction records where legally required.

We do **not**:

- Sell personal data to third parties.
- Use personal data to train AI models.
- Share with advertisers, data brokers, or analytics networks beyond
  the minimal sub-processors listed below.

By design, a **helper** you approve sees:

- Real-time screen content of your device while a session is active.
- Chat messages you send during that session.
- Session history for sessions you both participated in.

Helpers **do not** see your account email, password, device list, or
sessions they weren't part of. You can revoke a helper pairing at any
time from your dashboard.

### 4.3 Public authorities

We disclose data only when legally compelled (a valid subpoena, court
order, or equivalent) and will resist overly broad requests. We will
notify you of a request unless legally prohibited from doing so.

### 4.4 Corporate transactions

If KinBridge is ever acquired or merged, your data may transfer with
the service. We will notify you in advance and give you an opportunity
to delete your account before the transfer.

## 9. Security

We use industry-standard safeguards:

- **TLS 1.2+** for all network traffic to our dashboard and API.
- **Row-level security** (RLS) on all database tables — a user can only
  read rows they are party to, enforced by the database, not by
  application code.
- **Short-lived tokens** for session establishment (≤60 seconds).
- **Hardening** on our relay host (fail2ban, unattended-upgrades, CIS
  baseline, Lynis hardening index ≥70). See our public
  [SECURITY.md](https://github.com/sitothewild/kinbridge-server/blob/master/SECURITY.md).
- **Threat modeling** and an active
  [THREAT_MODEL.md](https://github.com/sitothewild/kinbridge-server/blob/master/THREAT_MODEL.md)
  that we update as the service evolves.

No system is perfect. If you discover a security issue please email
{{SECURITY_EMAIL}}; we treat security reports as a priority.
